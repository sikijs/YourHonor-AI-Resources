# Appendix A — AI Glossary

## Introduction

Throughout this book, you have encountered many new terms that are commonly used in artificial intelligence. This glossary provides simple, plain-English definitions that can serve as a quick reference whenever you encounter unfamiliar terminology.

---

# A

## Agent

An AI system that can pursue a goal by planning actions, using tools, gathering information, and making decisions.

Unlike a traditional chatbot that only responds to prompts, an agent can perform multiple steps to complete a task.

**Example:** An AI legal assistant that searches case law, summarizes findings, and drafts a memorandum.

---

## Algorithm

A set of instructions that tells a computer how to solve a problem or perform a task.

AI systems rely on algorithms to learn patterns from data.

**Example:** A recipe is an algorithm for cooking a meal.

---

## Artificial Intelligence (AI)

The field of creating computer systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.

Examples include language translation, image recognition, decision support, and content generation.

---

## Artificial Neural Network

A computing system inspired by the structure of the human brain.

It consists of connected units called neurons that process information and learn patterns from data.

---

# B

## Backpropagation

A learning process used by neural networks.

The system compares its prediction to the correct answer, calculates the error, and adjusts itself to improve future predictions.

**Simple idea:** Learn from mistakes.

---

## Bias

A systematic tendency toward certain outcomes.

Bias can originate from training data, human assumptions, or model design.

**Example:** If historical hiring data favors one group, an AI hiring system may learn that pattern.

---

# C

## Chatbot

A software application designed to conduct conversations with humans.

Modern chatbots are often powered by large language models.

---

## Context Window

The amount of information an AI model can consider at one time.

A larger context window allows the model to remember more of a conversation or document.

---

# D

## Data

Information used to train or operate AI systems.

Data may include text, images, audio, video, sensor readings, or numerical records.

**Example:** Court opinions, contracts, and legal briefs are forms of data.

---

## Deep Learning

A branch of machine learning that uses large neural networks with many layers.

Deep learning powers most modern AI systems.

---

# E

## Embedding

A numerical representation of information that allows AI systems to measure similarity and relationships.

Embeddings help models understand that related concepts are connected.

**Example:** "Attorney" and "lawyer" will have similar embeddings.

---

# F

## Fine-Tuning

The process of adapting a pre-trained AI model for a specialized purpose.

**Example:** Training a general AI model to perform legal research more effectively.

---

# G

## Generative AI

AI systems that create new content rather than simply analyze existing information.

Generated content may include:

* Text
* Images
* Audio
* Video
* Computer code

---

## GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

A specialized computer processor capable of performing many calculations simultaneously.

GPUs made modern deep learning practical.

---

# H

## Hallucination

An AI-generated statement that sounds convincing but is incorrect, misleading, or entirely fabricated.

Hallucinations are one of the most important limitations of current AI systems.

---

# L

## Large Language Model (LLM)

A neural network trained on massive amounts of text to predict and generate language.

Examples include systems such as ChatGPT and other modern AI assistants.

---

## Machine Learning

A branch of AI in which computers learn patterns from data rather than following explicitly programmed rules.

---

## Model

The trained AI system itself.

A model contains learned parameters that allow it to make predictions and generate outputs.

---

## Multimodal AI

An AI system capable of working with multiple types of information simultaneously.

Examples include:

* Text
* Images
* Audio
* Video

---

# N

## Neural Network

A system of interconnected artificial neurons that learns patterns from data.

Neural networks form the foundation of most modern AI systems.

---

# P

## Parameter

An internal numerical value learned during training.

Parameters collectively represent what an AI model has learned.

Modern models may contain billions or even trillions of parameters.

---

## Perceptron

One of the earliest artificial neurons.

Introduced by Frank Rosenblatt in the 1950s, it became an important milestone in AI history.

---

## Prompt

The instruction or question provided to an AI system.

**Example:**

```text
Summarize this contract in plain English.
```

The sentence above is a prompt.

---

# R

## Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

A technique that allows AI systems to retrieve external information before generating a response.

Rather than relying solely on what was learned during training, the model can consult documents, databases, or search results.

**Example:** A legal AI system searching recent court decisions before answering a question.

---

## Reinforcement Learning

A learning method in which an AI system improves through rewards and penalties.

Similar to how people learn from success and failure.

---

# T

## Token

A piece of text processed by an AI model.

Words may be represented as one token or multiple tokens depending on the language and context.

**Example:**

```text
Artificial Intelligence
```

might be split into multiple tokens before processing.

---

## Training

The process of teaching an AI model by exposing it to large amounts of data.

During training, the model gradually learns patterns and relationships.

---

## Transformer

A neural network architecture introduced in 2017 that revolutionized AI.

Transformers enable models to understand relationships between words and concepts across large amounts of text.

Most modern AI systems are based on transformer architectures.

---

# V

## Vector

A list of numbers used to represent information mathematically.

Embeddings are stored as vectors.

Vectors allow AI systems to compare meanings and relationships.

---

# W

## Weight

A numerical value that determines how strongly one artificial neuron influences another.

During training, weights are adjusted to improve performance.

---

# Final Thought

You do not need to memorize every term in this glossary.

The most important takeaway is that modern AI systems are built from a relatively small set of core ideas:

**Data → Training → Neural Networks → Models → Predictions**

Nearly every AI technology discussed in this book is a variation or extension of those foundational concepts.

As AI continues to evolve, new terminology will emerge, but understanding these fundamentals will allow you to navigate future developments with confidence.
